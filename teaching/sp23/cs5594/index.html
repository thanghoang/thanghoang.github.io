    Resources

    The best source for this course is research articles that can be found on Google or public repositories such as iacr or arxiv. Some preferred books for cryptography and blockchain are as follows.

    Blockchain Textbook
    • Bitcoin and Cryptocurrency Technologies. Arvind Narayanan, Joseph Bonneau, Edward Felten, Andrew Miller and Steven Goldfeder. Princeton University Press, 2016. (draft version here)
    • Foundations of Distributed Consensus and Blockchains (free). Elaine Shi. Book manuscript, 2020.
    Crypto Textbook
    • Introduction to Modern Cryptography (3rd edition). Jonathan Katz and Yehuda Lindell. Chapman and Hall/CRC, 2020.
    • A Graduate Course in Applied Cryptography (free). Dan Boneh and Victor Shoup. Book manuscript, 2020
    LaTeX Resources
      User Manual
    • LaTeX in 24 hours: a practical guide for scientific writing. Dilip Datta, Springer, 2017.
    • The Not So Short Introduction to LATEX2ε (v. 5.03). Tobias Oetiker Hubert Partl, Irene Hyna, and Elisabeth Schlegl, 2021.
    • How to Insert Graphics into LaTex Document
      • Software
    • Overleaf is a cloud-based LaTeX editor used for writing, editing and publishing scientific documents. Premium is free through University (required VT email).
    • MiKTeX is a free cross-platform TeX distribution for Windows, Linux and macOS.
    • MacTeX is a free redistribution of TeX Live for specific Mac users.
    • TeXstudio is a free cross-platform IDE to prepare LaTeX document on local machine (Windows, Linux, macOS).
